### When to Transition from NZ to Australian System

**Recommended transition windows (NZ → NSW):**

1. **End of NZ Year 6 → NSW Year 5 or 6 (age ~10–11):** Early enough to settle into the Australian primary system before secondary. Allows time to build friendships and adjust to the NSW curriculum before the academic intensity increases.

2. **End of NZ Year 8 → NSW Year 7 (age ~12–13) — natural breakpoint:** NZ Year 8 is the end of primary/intermediate. NSW Year 7 is the start of secondary. This is the cleanest structural alignment — both systems have a major transition at this point. Rachel could finish NZ primary and start NSW secondary with a fresh cohort.

3. **End of NZ Year 10 → NSW Year 9 (age ~14–15) — latest comfortable window:** Still early enough to complete 4 full years of NSW secondary (Years 9–12) and sit the HSC without disadvantage. Moving later than this risks curriculum gaps in HSC prerequisite subjects.

**Key considerations:**
- If university in Australia is the goal, completing **Years 11–12 in NSW** for the HSC is strongly recommended. The NCEA (NZ) is accepted by Australian universities but the ATAR conversion can be less favourable.
- **Year 7 in NSW** (= NZ Year 8) is the most popular transition point — it's a natural fresh start in a new school for all students.
- Moving mid-year is possible but starting at the **beginning of the school year** (late January in both countries) gives the best social integration.
- Both countries run a calendar-year school cycle (Jan–Dec), which simplifies timing.
